## The core thesis

The people Storyboard is built for are already organized. They're already gathering, already talking, already hitting the exact problem the product solves. You don't need to build an audience — you need to find where the audience already is and show up there authentically.

With the right approach, 10,000 users is achievable without paid acquisition.

---

## Pillar 1: AI community seeding

There are thousands of AI-specific communities that have never been reached with a product like this.

**What exists:**
- Facebook groups (Claude group = 1.2M members — and that's one group on one platform)
- Every AI meetup globally has its own Discord / WhatsApp / Telegram group
- If Valencia has one Claude meetup, there are probably 200+ globally
- Each meetup group: 50–500 people, all pre-qualified

**The math:**
200 meetup groups × 200 members = 40,000 people who are exactly the right fit, already organized, already talking about AI workflow problems.

Add the Facebook groups, Reddit communities, LinkedIn groups, Slack workspaces — you're looking at a reachable audience of millions with near-zero acquisition cost.

**Why this works:**
These aren't cold audiences. They're builders who respect someone who built something real. A genuine post — "I kept losing my Claude sessions so I built this" — lands completely differently than an ad. Authenticity is the only currency that works in these spaces.

**How AI makes it scalable:**
- Use AI to map and categorize communities (platform, size, focus, language)
- Draft context-specific messages for each community type
- Track which communities have been reached, what resonated, what to try next
- Storyboard tracks the strategy as it executes — the product demonstrates itself

## Pillar 2: Social listening — catch people in the moment

Anywhere on the internet, anyone who posts about:
- "My Claude context ran out again"
- "I lost all my AI memory"
- "Starting a new chat from zero is killing me"
- "I keep having to re-explain everything to the AI"

...is a warm lead. And that post is public. A good reply with a link gets seen by everyone who reads that thread.

**Platforms to monitor:**
- Twitter/X — real-time, high-visibility, often goes beyond original audience
- Reddit (r/ClaudeAI, r/ChatGPT, r/LocalLLaMA, r/productivity, dev subreddits)
- LinkedIn — professionals talking about AI workflow
- Hacker News — Show HN posts, comment threads
- Stack Overflow / dev forums
- YouTube comments under AI tutorial videos

**The approach:**
Not spam. A specific, useful reply: "This is exactly why I built [link] — it captures the session and carries it forward so you never start from zero." One sentence. One link. The rest is curiosity.

**Why this compounds:**
Each good reply is permanently visible. It gets indexed. It gets upvoted. Someone searching for "Claude context window solution" in 6 months finds your reply from today.

## On funding

The first constraint isn't infrastructure — it's message clarity and distribution reach. Both of those can be solved with AI before they need to be solved with money.

If community + social listening gets to 1,000 DAU, that changes the funding conversation entirely. You raise from traction, not promise. The pitch becomes: "We got 1,000 daily users with no ad spend. Here's what we'll do with capital."

What funding would actually unlock:
- Dedicated infrastructure for cloud sync + team features (the revenue tier)
- Ability to sponsor meetups, run events
- One technical person for scaled backend
- Paid distribution once the message is proven organic

The order matters: prove the message with community, then accelerate with money.
