# GTM — Two-Phase User Strategy

**Date:** Apr 18, 2026  
**Project:** Storyboard  
**Type:** Discussion  
**Status:** Strategic decision — locked for sprint  
**Produced:** Phase 1 target · Phase 2 hypothesis · Segmentation risk · Extension as consumer MVP

---

## The decision

Don't go broad immediately. Go deep first, then broad.

**Phase 1 — First 500 power users**  
Target: Claude Cowork users, Claude Projects users, people already spending 3+ hours per day in AI sessions. These people already feel the pain. The journey selector, MCP integration, context fault lines, seed modal — all of it lands with full force because they've lived through the problem it solves.

Goal: build a community of 500 people who become the product's first evangelists. These aren't just users — they're the feedback loop that sharpens the product before it goes broad.

**Phase 2 — Browser extension consumer test**  
Once the core community is solid, test the extension as a standalone consumer product. Lighter entry point: "You're using Claude — this saves your sessions." No dashboard required to get value.

The hypothesis: the extension might be the acquisition channel that brings casual users in, who then discover the deeper product. You're simultaneously testing whether the extension works as a consumer MVP.

---

## Why this sequencing is right

Power users are the most valuable first users because:
- They experience the pain most acutely (they have the most sessions to lose)
- They'll actually use the product deeply and find the real edges
- They'll talk about it in communities where other power users live
- Their use cases are the most interesting to build for
- 100 power users who love it beats 1,000 casual users who bounce

Getting this wrong (going broad before the product is hardened by power user feedback) means:
- Casual users arrive, get confused by complexity, leave bad reviews
- The brand gets tagged as "too complicated" before the consumer version exists
- The power users never find it because it looks like a toy

---

## The two products that are emerging

This framing clarifies something important: there may be two distinct products here.

**Product 1 — Storyboard (power user dashboard)**  
Dashboard + MCP + context fault lines + seed modal + categories + journey selector. Full workflow tool. Entry via the journey selector. Revenue tier: team features, cloud sync.

**Product 2 — Storyboard Widget (extension)**  
Browser extension only. "Capture any AI conversation in one click. Never explain context twice." Consumer product. Simpler onboarding. Low friction. Could be free forever with upsell to the full dashboard.

These might have different brand voice, different acquisition channels, maybe eventually different pricing tiers. The extension is the acquisition funnel. The dashboard is the product people stay for.

---

## Journey selector — Phase 1 only

The current journey selector (Running a business / Starting / Selling / Side project / Power user / Track chats) is power-user framing. These are the right journeys for Phase 1.

For Phase 2 casual users, the journey is just: "I use Claude and I keep losing my conversations." The extension widget is the product. The journey is: capture → see it in dashboard → gradually use more features.

Don't force casual users through a journey selector designed for power users. It's the wrong onboarding for them.

---

## The risk to avoid

Trying to serve both simultaneously with the same message.

Power users will bounce from consumer copy ("Never lose a conversation again" sounds too simple for someone who wants cross-project context compounding).

Casual users will bounce from power user copy ("Cross-model context seeding with three invisible layers" sounds overwhelming if you just want to save your ChatGPT chats).

**Phase 1 message:** "Built for AI power users who work across multiple sessions and tools."  
**Phase 2 message:** "Never start a conversation from scratch again."

Two messages. Two phases. One product underneath.

---

## Immediate actions

1. Phase 1 target list: Claude Cowork community, Claude Facebook group (1.2M members), AI meetup groups globally
2. Phase 1 onboarding: journey selector → full dashboard. This is what's being built.
3. Phase 2 when ready: extension landing page with single CTA, no journey selector, "Add to Chrome → capture your first session"
4. Test: what's the overlap? Do extension users convert to dashboard users without being pushed?
