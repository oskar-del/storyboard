## The idea

Target people who are almost certainly AI power users by profession. Not by finding them in AI communities — by finding them in the places they already work.

The logic: if you know someone is a freelance developer, a marketing consultant, or a solo lawyer, there's a high probability they're already using AI chat in their workflow. And if they're using AI chat, they're hitting the context problem. You don't need to qualify them — the profession is the qualifier.

**Rough math:**
- Freelancer.com alone: millions of active users
- Upwork: millions more
- LinkedIn "marketing consultant" / "freelance developer" searches: hundreds of thousands
- 5,000,000 emails × 1% click rate × 2% sign-up = 1,000 users from one campaign
- At a more realistic 0.5% conversion: still 2,500 users

Even a small fraction of a large number is large.

## Target persona map

Industries most likely to be AI coworker users:

**Tier 1 — Near certainty:**
- Freelance developers (Upwork, Toptal, Freelancer.com)

- UX/UI designers
- Content creators, copywriters
- Solo founders / startup teams

**Tier 2 — High probability:**
- Lawyers (solo practices, boutique firms)
- Accountants / financial consultants
- Recruiters (heavy AI usage since 2023)
- Research analysts
- Sales professionals at tech companies

**Tier 3 — Growing:**
- Real estate agents (new category, rapidly adopting)

- HR professionals

## The compliance question

Scraping emails directly from platforms like Freelancer or Upwork is:
- Against most platforms' Terms of Service
- Potentially in violation of GDPR (EU) and CAN-SPAM (US) for unsolicited contact
- Risky for brand reputation — early-stage products get flagged as spam permanently

This doesn't kill the idea — it kills one execution path. The insight (target by profession, not by community) is solid.

---

## The compliant version of the same idea

**Apollo.io / Hunter.io** — databases of professional contact info where individuals have given general consent to receive business outreach. Filterable by job title, company size, industry. Paid but legal.

**LinkedIn outreach** — Sales Navigator lets you target by exact criteria (job title, industry, company size, location). Direct messages feel more personal than email. Higher response rate per contact.

**Facebook/LinkedIn Ads with lead gen form** — instead of cold email, run a hyper-targeted paid campaign to the same audience. $10 CPM, 2% CTR, 1% form fill = $500/1000 leads. Not free, but trackable.

**Warm version** — build the list from people who already showed signal: replied to a tweet about context windows, commented on an AI tutorial, participated in an AI community. Smaller list, much higher conversion.

---

## The right sequencing

The mass email idea is a Phase 2 play, not Phase 1.

Phase 1: community seeding + social listening (organic, zero cost, proves message)
Phase 2: once the message is proven → targeted outreach via compliant databases or paid distribution
Phase 3: at scale → partnerships with platforms (Notion, Figma, Raycast) that already have the right users

The professional targeting insight is real and should be used — but with compliant tools once the message is locked.
